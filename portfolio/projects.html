My Projects

1. NIL Case Study for Strategic Management of Sport Organizations

For this case study, I discussed what NIL (Name, Image, and Likeness) is, and how to improve it. I looked at three main issues: Restrictions and Regulations, Equity, and Competitive Imbalance. Next, I came up with solutions to these issues. I had to research what exactly has gone wrong, and come up with realistic ways the NCAA may resolve these problems. While I do not intend on working in college athletics, this project was worthwhile because it helped me understand important issues in the industry. NIL will also have a huge impact on professional sports, as more and more athletes will stay in school longer due to the money they can make through NIL.

2. Miami Marlins Case Study for Organizational Behavior of Sport Organizations

This was a very interesting project I worked on last year. Along with three classmates, we broke down what has gone wrong with the Miami Marlinsâ€™ management, and why this might be. In 2020, they hired the first female General Manager in the big four American sports leagues, but within three years, she stepped down. Their CEO, Derek Jeter, had also just left the organization. As a group, we looked into possible causes of this, while also working on a solution to fix it. To us, there is a clear issue at the top of management, which is why we think it is best for the Marlins to completely change their structure. We created a new management tree, so everyone knows who is in charge. With this system in place, no one should feel undercut, and everyone will be on the same page.
